HIV & AIDS Needs Assessment



   This survey, the HIV Needs Assessment, was conducted through CHOW, The Hawaii Life Foundation and The Hawaii Department of Health in 2017. There are 398 participants hailing from four of the eight Hawaiian Islands. Dr. Barile and Joy Agner, from the University of Hawaii at Manoa, analyzed the data and visualizations were created by Team Treatment of HIV and AIDS in Hawaiâ€™i from the University of Hawaii at Manoa.

There is a large discrepancy between the HIV Needs Assessment and the Epi Profile. The HIV Needs Assessment has a much larger amount of people 55 and over, and a much smaller amount for all age groups from 25 to 55 compared to the Epi Profile.
The Epi Profile has no data on Case Management Providers. However, a large proportion of people from the HIV Needs Assessment are receiving case management from the Life Foundation.
The Epi Profile has no data on disabilities. However, a large proportion of people from the HIV Needs Assessment suffered from heart disease or some form of mental health disorder.
The Epi Profile has no data on drug use. However, a large proportion of the drug-using people from the HIV Assessment were meth or marijuana users.
The Epi Profile has no data on education level. Most people from the HIV Needs Assessment went to at least some amount of college, but it is shown most people either did not go to or did not finish college.
The Epi Profile has no data on income types. However, most people from the HIV Needs Assessment had SSI/SSDI as theire primary income.
There were no significant differences between the methods of transmission of the HIV Needs Assessment and the Epi Profile. However, a large amount of people received HIV through Male to Male Sexual Contact over anything else.
The HIV Needs Assessment and the Epi Profile have a number of small discrepancies mostly in the range of about 5%. It must be noted that anyone who was multiple Asian races or multiple Pacific Islander races ONLY in the HIV Needs Assessment was counted as a mixed-race person, but it is unclear how the Epi Profile counted its mixed race people. Other than that, the HIV Needs Assessment has a much larger percentage of Multiple Race people, which may further support this issue. In general, there are a large amount of white-only people compared to any other race in either distribution.
The Epi Profile has no data on sexual orientation. However, a large amount of people from the HIV Needs Assessment were male homosexuals.

While it can be difficult for patients with AIDS/HIV to find and access good care, in Hawaii many people are able to obtain the services they need for the most part. However, there is still a long way to go to ensure that all patients are able to get the services to live a better life.

The top barrier is that most respondents could not afford alternative therapy.
The age group that has the most respondents who answered "very difficult" or "too difficult" are those aged 55 and up.
The majority of the respondents do not use any illegal substances.

The majority of the respondents' highest level of education was "some college".

The main source of income for these respondents is SSI/SSDI.

The majority of respodents cited "sex with men" as the most likely way they recieved HIV.

The majority of the respondents that found it difficult to access alternative therapy were White.

There seems to be a majority of homosexual males who find it difficult to access alternative therapy.
